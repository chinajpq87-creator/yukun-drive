## Why Slotless BLDC?

Conventional BLDC motors use a slotted stator — iron teeth around which copper windings are wrapped. This creates **cogging torque**: a jerky, stepped feel as the rotor magnets align with stator teeth.

```
SLOTTED STATOR                          SLOTLESS STATOR
┌─────────────────────┐                ┌─────────────────────┐
│  ╔═══╗ ╔═══╗ ╔═══╗  │                │  ┌───────────────┐  │
│  ║ █ ║ ║ █ ║ ║ █ ║  │  ← iron teeth │  │  ░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  │  │
│  ╚═══╝ ╚═══╝ ╚═══╝  │     cogging    │  │  ░ copper ░░░  │  │
│   ↑     ↑     ↑      │                │  │  ░  coil   ░░  │  │
│  cogging at each slot │                │  │  ░░░░░░░░░░░░  │  │
└─────────────────────┘                └─────────────────────┘
    ❌ Jerky at low speed                  ✅ Smooth at any speed
    ❌ Audible vibration                   ✅ <30dB
    ❌ Higher EMI                          ✅ Minimal EMI
```

| Feature | Slotted BLDC | Slotless BLDC (BL-24C) |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Cogging Torque | Yes — stepped feel | **Zero** — perfectly smooth |
| Low-Speed Performance | Jerky | **Silky smooth** |
| Noise | 40-55 dB | **<30 dB** |
| EMI | Moderate | **Minimal** |
| Efficiency | Slightly higher | **Slightly lower** (5-10% less) |
| Cost | Lower | **Higher** |
| Best For | High-volume, cost-sensitive | **Medical, precision, quiet** |

> 💡 Choose slotless when smoothness, silence, and low EMI matter more than a 5-10% efficiency difference.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need a controller to run this motor?**
A: Yes. All BLDC motors require an external electronic controller to commutate the phases. We offer compatible controllers — or you can use your own. The Hall sensor version works with any standard BLDC controller.

**Q: What's the difference between sensor-based and sensorless?**
A: Sensor-based (Hall) provides reliable startup and low-speed performance. Sensorless is simpler wiring (3 wires vs 8) but can be jerky at startup. For medical and precision applications, we recommend sensor-based with Hall sensors.

**Q: Can this motor run directly on DC power?**
A: No — this is a brushless DC motor, not a brushed DC motor. It requires a BLDC controller to drive the 3-phase windings in sequence. The "DC" in BLDC refers to the DC bus voltage, not direct connection.

**Q: Is the 15,000 hour life guaranteed?**
A: The 15,000 hour rating is bearing life (L10) under rated load and temperature. The motor itself (windings, magnets) has no inherent wear mechanism — unlike brushed motors. Actual life depends on operating conditions.

**Q: What makes it "slotless"?**
A: The stator has no iron teeth. Copper windings are formed into a self-supporting cylindrical structure and bonded in place. This eliminates all cogging torque and iron losses — the trade-off is slightly lower efficiency and higher manufacturing cost.

**Q: Can I use this in a surgical robot?**
A: Yes. The zero cogging, <30dB noise, and minimal EMI make it well-suited for surgical applications. We recommend the FOC controller for precision torque/position control. Contact us for medical-grade documentation and biocompatible material options.
