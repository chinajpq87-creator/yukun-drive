## Why Self-Locking Matters

The worm gear geometry provides a unique mechanical property: the worm (motor side) can drive the gear (output side), but the gear **cannot** back-drive the worm.

```
     MOTOR DRIVES OUTPUT          EXTERNAL FORCE ON OUTPUT
     ┌──────┐                    ┌──────┐
     │ MOTOR│  ⚡ rotation       │ MOTOR│  🔒 LOCKED
     │  ⚙️   │  ──────→          │  ⚙️   │  ✗────
     │  🪱   │                    │  🪱   │   ← force
     └──┬───┘                    └──┬───┘
        │  ✅ MOVES                  │  ❌ CANNOT MOVE
        ↓                           ↓
     [OUTPUT]                    [OUTPUT]
```

**This means:** Your mechanism stays locked without consuming any power — fail-safe by physics, not by software.

| Application | Why Self-Locking Matters |
|:---|:---|
| 🔌 EV Charging Gun Lock | Connector must stay locked during charging even if vehicle loses power. IEC 62196/GB/T 20234 requirement. |
| 🔐 High-Security Deadbolt | Bolt cannot be pushed back from outside — physical anti-tamper security. |
| 🏥 Medical Lift/Positioner | Load must hold position safely when power is off. |
| ⚙️ Valve Actuator | Valve must maintain position without continuous power draw. |
| 🚗 Automotive Actuator | Mechanism must hold position under vibration and shock. |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the self-locking force guaranteed?**
A: Yes. Every batch is tested for back-drive resistance at the rated self-locking force. The worm gear geometry inherently provides this property — it is not dependent on electronics or control software.

**Q: Can the motor be driven in both directions?**
A: Yes. The motor runs in both directions. The self-locking property is on the **output side** — the output cannot be back-driven, but the motor can drive it both clockwise and counterclockwise.

**Q: What happens at end-of-travel or stall?**
A: We recommend a limit switch (micro switch) or current-sensing cut-off in your control circuit. At stall, the motor draws ~500mA (12V). Our integrated micro switch assemblies provide mechanical end-of-travel detection.

**Q: How does this compare to the N20 Metal Spur?**
A: Worm gear = self-locking, 90° output axis, higher reduction per stage, slightly lower efficiency. Spur gear = non-locking, parallel output, higher efficiency, lower cost. Choose worm for safety-critical holding; choose spur for cost-optimized continuous motion.

**Q: What's the maximum operating temperature?**
A: Standard: -30°C to +85°C. High-temperature variants (special grease + winding) available up to +105°C for under-hood automotive applications.

**Q: Can the motor be used outdoors in rain/snow?**
A: The motor itself is not fully sealed. For outdoor use (charging guns, external locks), we recommend the ASM-01 assembly with IP67-sealed micro switch and sealed housing. The gearbox can be specified with sealed bearings and waterproof grease.

**Q: What's the expected cycle life?**
A: Standard: 50,000+ cycles under rated load. Extended life version: 200K+ cycles validated (reinforced gear, premium lubrication). Specify your life requirement when requesting a sample.
