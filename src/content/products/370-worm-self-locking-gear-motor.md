## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When should I choose the 370 over the N20 worm motor?**
A: Choose the 370 when your application requires more than 10 kg·cm of driving torque or 12 kg·cm of holding force. The N20 is ideal for AC charging guns and compact locks. The 370 is for DC fast charging and industrial applications where brute force matters.

**Q: Can the 370 run on 12V vehicle power directly?**
A: Yes. The 12V winding is designed for direct automotive 12V electrical systems. Nominal 12V (9-16V range), with transient protection available. The 24V option is for commercial vehicles and industrial DC systems.

**Q: Is the self-locking force really 36 kg·cm?**
A: Yes, at 1000:1 reduction. The self-locking force scales with reduction ratio — higher ratio = higher holding force. Every batch is tested for back-drive resistance at the rated specification.

**Q: What's the operating temperature range?**
A: Standard: -30°C to +85°C. This covers outdoor charging stations in most climates. For extreme cold (Nordic regions) or extreme heat (desert/Middle East), we offer special grease and winding configurations.

**Q: How does this integrate with a charging gun handle?**
A: The motor is typically mounted inside the charging gun handle or in the connector body. We provide 3D CAD models for your integration design. The ASM-02 assembly includes mounting features for direct installation.
