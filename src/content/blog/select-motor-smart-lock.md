# How to Select Micro Gear Motors for Smart Door Locks

The motor is the single most critical component in a smart lock. It determines reliability, battery life, security, and user experience. Here's how to choose the right one.

## The 4 Critical Factors

### 1. Battery Life
Smart locks operate on 4×AA batteries for 6-12 months. Your motor must draw minimal current.
- **Target:** <80mA no-load, <400mA stall
- **Recommendation:** N20 Metal Spur, 6V winding

### 2. Noise
Nobody wants a loud lock on their front door.
- **Target:** <50dB at 30cm
- **Recommendation:** Precision-ground metal spur gears

### 3. Anti-Tamper Security
Can an attacker push the bolt back from outside? If your lock mechanism can be back-driven, you have a security vulnerability.
- **Solution:** Worm gear self-locking motor — cannot be back-driven by design
- **Recommendation:** N20 Worm Self-Lock, 200:1

### 4. Cycle Life
Hotel locks need 100K+ cycles. Residential locks: 20K-50K.
- **Standard:** N20 Metal Spur: 50K+ cycles
- **Extended:** N20 Planetary: 100K+ cycles
- **Maximum:** N20 Planetary + premium lubrication: 200K+ cycles

## Quick Selection Table

| Lock Type | Recommended Motor | Key Reason |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Residential fingerprint (cost-sensitive) | N20 Metal Spur 100:1, 6V | Best value, proven |
| Premium fingerprint (smooth, quiet) | N20 Planetary 64:1, 12V | Low backlash, premium feel |
| High-security deadbolt (anti-tamper) | N20 Worm Self-Lock 200:1 | Cannot be back-driven |
| Hotel lock (high cycle) | N20 Planetary 64:1 + Hall Encoder | 200K+ rated, position feedback |
